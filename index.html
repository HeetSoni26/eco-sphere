What is a Carbon Footprint?

A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases — primarily CO₂ — produced directly and indirectly by an individual, organization, or event. Understanding it is the first step toward reducing it.

Energy

Burning fossil fuels for electricity & heat is the largest single source of global emissions (~34%).

Transport

Transportation accounts for ~16% of global emissions. Switching to EVs or public transit is one of the highest-impact changes.

Diet

Beef production emits 20x more CO₂ than plant protein. Going vegan could cut your food footprint by over 70%.

Waste

Landfills produce methane — a greenhouse gas 80x more potent than CO₂ over 20 years. Reduce, reuse, recycle.

Global Context

  • 🌡️ Global average temperature is already 1.1°C above pre-industrial levels.
  • 🎯 The Paris Agreement target is to stay below 1.5°C of warming.
  • 👤 The average Indian emits ~1.9 Tons of CO₂/year vs. ~16 Tons for an American.
  • 🌱 Planting 1 tree sequesters approximately 21 kg of CO₂ per year.
